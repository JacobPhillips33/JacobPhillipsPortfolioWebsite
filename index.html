bio

I am a Petroleum Engineer turned Full-Stack Software Engineer who specializes in a variety of languages and frameworks including ASP.NET Core MVC, C#, SQL, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. What I love most about coding is how it gives me the ability to create applications that improve our lives. I enjoy taking complex problems, breaking them down into manageable parts, and then turning the process into an application that will solve this problem automatically from now on!

While I have fun developing some projects on my own, I find it highly rewarding to collaborate with and learn from other individuals who share my passion. I have discovered that the experience and growth that comes from working with a team is invaluable, and I look forward to each time as an opportunity to hone and enhance my technical coding skills as well as a chance to improve upon my ability to effectively communicate with everyone involved.

When I'm not at my computer you can find me hiking with my wife and dog, golfing, or learning more about coding (I'm slightly obsessed). I am proud to be a Petroleum Engineer turned Software Engineer and wish I had made the change sooner. Thank you for taking the time to read about my journey. I would love to hear about yours.

If you are interested in reading further, click here for a quick background about my coding life.

I was first introduced to coding in college when I took a Visual Basic course. I liked it so much I wanted to switch majors, but, unfortunately, the Colorado School of Mines did not have a Computer Science degree at that time. Rather than transferring to a different college, I went on to graduate with a Bachelor of Science degree in Petroleum Engineering.

I moved on with my career, but I always thought, "If I could do things over, I would have gotten my degree in Computer Science." I continued to dabble with coding over the years but did not seriously consider making a switch until I was introduced to coding bootcamps. I took the leap and signed up for TrueCoders Full-Stack Software Engineering Bootcamp and am so glad that I did. The passion that was sparked during my Visual Basic class turned into a burning flame. I found that I truly love coding, especially the back-end development areas.

TrueCoders was an amazing experience. In addition to giving me a great coding foundation, I walked away with over fifty projects, a portfolio website, and a passion for coding. During this course I found myself constantly pushing to not just complete my projects, but to fully understand each line of code, how it worked, and why it was there. This passion keeps me moving forward, and I am excited to continue to learn and grow.

Geolocation - TacoParser

C# Console Application

Provided a CSV file containing Taco Bell locations in Alabama, this application will determine which two Taco Bell's are farthest apart and provide their distance apart in miles. It accomplishes this by parsing through the CSV file for each location's latitude and longitude, converting the latitude and longitude into a Geocoordinate, and comparing the distance between all of the Geocoordinates. In addition, this app utilized logging and TDD to to assist with the coding process.

Sudoku Solution Validator

C# Console Application

This C# application will evaluate a completed Sudoku Solution to determine whether it valid. It will return “True” if it is a valid solution or “False” if it is not. Sudoku is a game played on a 9x9 grid. The goal of the game is to fill all cells of the grid with digits from 1 to 9, so that each column, each row, and each of the nine 3x3 sub-grids (also known as blocks) contain all the digits from 1 to 9.
